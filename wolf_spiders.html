Fun Facts About Wolf Spiders:

  • Wolf spiders are agile and robust hunters that stalk and ambush their prey rather than building webs to catch them.
  • Female wolf spiders are devoted mothers; they carry their egg sac attached to their spinnerets until the spiderlings hatch.
  • After hatching, the tiny spiderlings climb onto their mother's back and ride there for several days to weeks, protected by her.
  • They possess excellent eyesight, particularly their two large forward-facing eyes, which aid them in hunting.
  • Wolf spiders are found in a wide variety of habitats, from deserts to rainforests, and are common in gardens and grasslands.
  • While their bite can be painful, similar to a bee sting, wolf spiders are generally not considered dangerous to humans.
  • They are nocturnal predators, often seen scurrying across the ground at night when hunting for insects and other small invertebrates.
