All About Spiders

Fun Facts About Spiders

  • Spiders are arachnids, not insects. They have eight legs and two body segments.
  • Not all spiders spin webs. Some hunt their prey, while others burrow.
  • Spider silk is one of the strongest natural fibers known.
  • There are over 45,000 known species of spiders worldwide.
  • Most spiders are harmless to humans, and their venom is not strong enough to cause serious harm.
  • Some spiders can live for several years, especially larger species like tarantulas.

Types of Spiders (Common Examples)

  • Orb-weaver spiders (known for their classic spiral webs)
  • Jumping spiders (excellent eyesight and hunting skills)
  • Wolf spiders (fast ground hunters)
  • Tarantulas (large, hairy, and often kept as pets)
  • Black Widows (known for their potent venom, though bites are rare)
  • Brown Recluse (also venomous, found in specific regions)
